Are your forensic reports court-admissible? +

Yes. Every report is prepared under § 65B of the Indian Evidence Act, with twin-examiner review, full chain-of-custody documentation and reproducible hashing. We've testified in over 80 matters across district, sessions and high courts.

Can you recover deleted WhatsApp / Signal messages? +

In most cases, yes. Recovery depends on the OS, encryption mode, time since deletion and whether cloud sync was enabled. We image, parse and reconstruct from SQLite remnants and unallocated space using Cellebrite, MSAB and our own tooling.

Do you preserve chain of custody? +

Always. Tamper-evident seals, dual-signed handover forms, photographed acquisitions and SHA-256 verification at every handoff. Our COC logs survive cross-examination.

How fast can you respond to an active intrusion? +

Our 24/7 incident line dispatches a triage analyst within 30 minutes; an on-site team within 4 hours in Tier 1 cities, 12 hours elsewhere in India.

Do you provide expert witness testimony? +

Yes. Six of our principal examiners are court-recognised expert witnesses. Briefs include written affidavit, cross-prep, exhibit walkthroughs and live testimony.

01 — Definition

What is mobile forensics?

Mobile forensics is the science of extracting, preserving and analysing data from mobile devices in a manner that is legally defensible. It covers active, deleted, encrypted and cloud-synced artefacts — and it requires equipment, training and process that off-the-shelf data-recovery tools cannot match.

"Recovered 14 deleted chats from a wiped iPhone — conviction in 9 months."

A senior R&D engineer at a listed manufacturer was suspected of exfiltrating proprietary firmware blueprints. The device had been wiped before seizure; the suspect denied possessing any IP at the time of resignation.

01 — Brief

The brief.

Counsel briefed us at 22:00 hrs on a Friday. The suspect was due to fly out 48 hours later. We dispatched a triage analyst overnight to the client's Andheri office to image three devices: a corporate iPhone 14 Pro, a personal Samsung S23, and a MacBook Air.

02 — Approach

What we did.

Chip-off was unnecessary — the iPhone had been factory-reset, not destroyed, leaving unallocated APFS regions intact. We imaged with Cellebrite UFED, parsed in Magnet AXIOM, then ran custom SQLite carving against the iMessage and WhatsApp shards.

The Samsung yielded a partial Signal SQLite remnant. We cross-referenced timestamps against the suspect's iCloud backups (court-ordered) and built a 6-week communication timeline.

03 — Outcome

The outcome.

14 deleted WhatsApp chats recovered, two of which contained CAD attachments matching the client's protected designs. The Mumbai sessions court admitted the § 65B certificate without dilution. Conviction in nine months. Suspect served 14 months.
